## Active Recall

**Q1: What is the difference between `<dl>`, `<dt>` and `<dd>`, and when would you use a definition list over a regular `<ul>` or `<ol>`?** A: The element `<dl>` stands for Description List and Is used to Semantically Order Items with a Sort of Header First (`<dt>`) and then an indented description underneath (`<dd>`). You would use this type of List when you want a List which Is Ordered More Semantically for Accessibility While Also Making it more Visually Appealing and Easier to Read WITHOUT the Need of CSS.

--

**Q2: What is the difference between `<q>` and `<blockquote>`? When do you use each one?** A: The tag `<q>` Is used to Semantically turn a Piece of Text into Italics and Turn it into a Quote so it's more semantic for Accessibility. The tag `<blockquote>` is for when you want to make a text which the whole thing is a Quote so then the Text inside of the BlockQuote is Indented and also In Italics while still being seen as a Quote for Accessibility. You would use `<q>` when you want to Turn text inside of other Text into Quotes (`<p>She said <q>I quite like the dogs,</q> happily </p>` e.g.) while `<blockquote>` is used to make the whole Text a quote.

--

**Q3: What does the `cite` attribute do on a `<blockquote>` tag, and is it visible to the user?** A: The `cite` attribute is used to declare the author or origin of a quote or article mentioned in the form of, for example, a URL. It is good as it's semantic, has good accessibility and it also Increases the SEO of a Website. It is not visible to the User.

--

**Q4: What does the `<cite>` element represent and how is it different from the `cite` attribute?** A: The `<cite>` element is used to Identify the Title of Work (e.g. The title of a Book). It is different to the `cite` Attribute as the Attribute is not Visible while the `<cite>` tag Is.
